Project 01 / 10
New Kansas City Chiefs Stadium
Wyandotte County, Kansas  ·  Opening 2031

One of the most significant stadium projects in NFL history, the new Chiefs domed stadium represents a landmark relocation from Arrowhead Stadium. As Principal Computational Designer, the work centers on the geometric resolution of the dome's structural envelope — a complex interplay of translucent cladding, long-span roof trusses, and parametric logic governing facade panelization at scale.

Custom Grasshopper tooling was developed to automate the iterative analysis of structural load paths across the dome geometry, enabling rapid design iteration between architectural intent and engineering performance. The $3 billion facility is designed to host Super Bowls, NCAA Final Fours, and major entertainment events.

Project 03 / 10
New Nissan Stadium — Tennessee Titans
Nashville, Tennessee

The new Nissan Stadium is a 60,000-seat enclosed facility featuring a signature translucent roof — one of the defining architectural elements requiring intensive computational resolution. As Principal Computational Designer, the work focused on the panelization logic of the translucent roof membrane, optimizing panel geometry for fabrication efficiency while maintaining structural performance required for the long-span enclosure.

Python-based automation tools were developed to interface with structural analysis software, enabling iterative optimization of the roof's framing system in response to load case scenarios.

Project 04 / 10
GEODIS Park — Nashville SC
Nashville, Tennessee

GEODIS Park is the largest soccer-specific stadium in North America at 30,000 seats — a landmark facility setting a new benchmark for MLS venue design. As Principal Computational Designer, the work involved the computational resolution of the stadium's structural canopy, facade geometry, and the complex bowl form that delivers unobstructed sightlines from every seat.

The project required tight integration between architectural geometry and structural framing, with custom Grasshopper workflows developed to automate the coordination of steel connection geometry across the canopy.

Project 05 / 10
CITIC Tower
Beijing, China  ·  528 Meters

CITIC Tower — known locally as China Zun — is a 528-meter supertall and the tallest building in Beijing. Designed by KPF, the tower's form is inspired by the ancient Chinese ritual bronze vessel Zun, with a profile that widens toward the top — an inversion of the conventional tapering skyscraper silhouette.

As Architectural Designer at KPF, contributions included the development and coordination of 3D design documentation, client presentation models, and the computational resolution of the tower's complex facade geometry across a form that rotates and expands simultaneously as it rises.

111 Murray Street
New York, New York

111 Murray Street is a luxury residential supertall in lower Manhattan, designed by KPF with interiors by David Rockwell. The tower's distinctive form — wider at the top than the bottom — is defined by a series of elegant horizontal setbacks and a subtle taper that gives the building its sculptural profile against the Manhattan skyline.

As Architectural Designer at KPF, contributions included the development of 2D and 3D documentation packages and the coordination of the tower's complex curved glass curtain wall geometry across floor plates of varying form.

Project 07 / 10
Parc Olympique — Roof Replacement
Montreal, Quebec, Canada

The Parc Olympique — Montreal's iconic 1976 Olympic Stadium — is one of the most architecturally complex buildings in North America, defined by its inclined concrete tower and cable-stayed roof. The roof replacement project undertakes the structural and enclosure redesign of this landmark facility.

As Structural and Enclosure Computational Designer at Walter P Moore, contributions centered on the computational modeling of the roof's cable-stayed structural system and the geometric resolution of the new enclosure elements, requiring deep engagement with the existing curved concrete shell geometry.

Project 10 / 10
Tiny Life Wiki — Off-Grid Tiny Home
Kansas City, Missouri

The Tiny Life Wiki project is a fully off-grid tiny home on wheels — a personal design-build project undertaken through PaperSpace.INK that represents a deliberate exploration of alternative construction typologies and minimal living. The project applies the same rigor as large-scale work: precise structural thinking, thoughtful spatial planning, and systems integration — at the intimate scale of a single dwelling.

Delivered end-to-end as Architect and Construction Manager: from initial concept through procurement, fabrication, and on-site construction. Off-grid systems including solar power, rainwater collection, and composting waste management were integrated from the outset.

Project 01 / 10
New Kansas City Chiefs Stadium
Wyandotte County, Kansas · Opening 2031

One of the most significant stadium projects in NFL history, the new Chiefs domed stadium represents a landmark relocation from the iconic Arrowhead Stadium in Missouri. As Principal Computational Designer, the work centers on the geometric resolution of the dome's structural envelope — a complex interplay of translucent cladding, long-span roof trusses, and the parametric logic governing facade panelization at scale.

Custom Grasshopper tooling was developed to automate the iterative analysis of structural load paths across the dome geometry, enabling rapid design iteration between architectural intent and engineering performance. The $3 billion facility is designed to host Super Bowls, NCAA Final Fours, and major entertainment events beyond football.

New Highmark Stadium — Buffalo Bills
Orchard Park, New York · Opening 2026

The new Highmark Stadium for the Buffalo Bills is a 62,000-seat open-air facility and the largest NFL construction project currently under delivery. As Principal Computational Designer at Walter P Moore, contributions centered on the structural and enclosure logic of the stadium's canopy system and the parametric coordination of the building's complex geometry across disciplines.

Custom automation pipelines were developed to manage the translation of architectural geometry into structural analysis models, enabling real-time feedback loops between the design and engineering teams. The stadium is designed for the rigors of Buffalo's climate while delivering a world-class fan experience.

Project 03 / 10
New Nissan Stadium — Tennessee Titans
Nashville, Tennessee

The new Nissan Stadium for the Tennessee Titans is a 60,000-seat enclosed facility featuring a signature translucent roof — one of the defining architectural elements that required intensive computational resolution. As Principal Computational Designer, the work focused on the panelization logic of the translucent roof membrane, optimizing panel geometry for fabrication efficiency while maintaining the structural performance required for the long-span enclosure.

Python-based automation tools were developed to interface with structural analysis software, enabling iterative optimization of the roof's framing system in response to load case scenarios. The stadium sits on the Cumberland River and is a centerpiece of Nashville's sports and entertainment landscape.

Project 04 / 10
GEODIS Park — Nashville SC
Nashville, Tennessee

GEODIS Park is the largest soccer-specific stadium in North America at 30,000 seats — a landmark facility that set a new benchmark for MLS venue design. As Principal Computational Designer, the work involved the computational resolution of the stadium's structural canopy, facade geometry, and the complex bowl form that delivers unobstructed sightlines from every seat.

The project required tight integration between architectural geometry and structural framing systems, with custom Grasshopper workflows developed to automate the coordination of steel connection geometry across the canopy. GEODIS Park has been widely celebrated for its acoustic performance and fan atmosphere, and was instrumental in establishing Nashville as a major-league sports city.

Project 05 / 10
CITIC Tower
Beijing, China · 528 Meters

CITIC Tower — known locally as China Zun — is a 528-meter supertall skyscraper and the tallest building in Beijing. Designed by Kohn Pedersen Fox, the tower's form is inspired by the ancient Chinese ritual bronze vessel Zun, with a profile that widens toward the top — an inversion of the conventional tapering skyscraper silhouette.

As Architectural Designer at KPF, contributions included the development and coordination of 3D design documentation, client presentation models, and the computational resolution of the tower's complex facade geometry. The project demanded precision in the coordination of curtain wall geometry across a form that rotates and expands simultaneously as it rises, requiring sophisticated parametric modeling strategies.

111 Murray Street
New York, New York

111 Murray Street is a luxury residential supertall in lower Manhattan, designed by Kohn Pedersen Fox with interiors by David Rockwell. The tower's distinctive form — wider at the top than the bottom — is defined by a series of elegant horizontal setbacks and a subtle taper that gives the building its distinctive sculptural profile against the Manhattan skyline.

As Architectural Designer at KPF, contributions included the development of 2D and 3D documentation packages and the coordination of the tower's complex facade geometry. The building's curved glass curtain wall required precise parametric coordination to achieve the smooth transitions between floor plates of varying geometry.

Project 07 / 10
Parc Olympique — Roof Structure Replacement
Montreal, Quebec, Canada

The Parc Olympique — Montreal's iconic 1976 Olympic Stadium — is one of the most architecturally complex buildings in North America, defined by its inclined concrete tower and the retractable Kevlar roof that has long been a source of engineering challenges. The roof replacement project undertakes the structural and enclosure redesign of this landmark facility.

As Structural and Enclosure Computational Designer at Walter P Moore, contributions centered on the computational modeling of the roof's cable-stayed structural system and the geometric resolution of the new enclosure elements. The project required deep engagement with the existing building geometry — a complex curved concrete shell — and the development of parametric tools to model the interaction between new and existing structural systems.

Suzhou International Finance Square
Suzhou, China

The Suzhou International Finance Square is a mixed-use supertall development in Suzhou's Suzhou Industrial Park — one of China's most ambitious urban development zones. Designed by KPF, the project comprises twin towers of varying heights with a shared podium, delivering hotel, office, retail, and serviced apartment programs across an iconic skyline composition.

As Architectural Designer, contributions included the development of the towers' facade documentation, 3D modeling of the complex curtain wall geometry, and coordination of construction document packages for international delivery. The project's scale and the precision required in the documentation of supertall facade systems informed a rigorous approach to parametric modeling and drawing production.

YTL Headquarters
Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

The YTL Headquarters is a corporate tower for one of Malaysia's most prominent conglomerates, sited in Kuala Lumpur's competitive skyline. Designed by Kohn Pedersen Fox, the building balances the formal language of KPF's global portfolio with the climatic and cultural context of Southeast Asia.

Project 10 / 10
Tiny Life Wiki — Off-Grid Tiny Home
Kansas City, Missouri

The Tiny Life Wiki project is a fully off-grid tiny home on wheels — a personal design-build project undertaken through PaperSpace.INK that represents a deliberate exploration of alternative construction typologies and minimal living. The project embodies the same rigor applied to large-scale work — precise structural thinking, thoughtful spatial planning, and systems integration — at the intimate scale of a single dwelling.

As Architectural Designer and Construction Manager, the project was delivered end-to-end: from initial concept and structural design through procurement, fabrication, and on-site construction. Off-grid systems including solar power, rainwater collection, and composting waste management were integrated into the design from the outset, making the home fully self-sufficient.
